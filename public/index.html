Snapshot export

Take the selected range with you

A Worker cron trigger stores minute-by-minute EMF snapshots in D1. Any selected range can be downloaded as raw JSON, CSV, or an Excel-compatible workbook, and the same range can be imported into Google Sheets.

In the fictional story tied to this site, ghosts are actively making the EMF reader go off, and even if the clean usability of the data remains questionable, a large enough EMF-390 dataset could still capture subtle fluctuations from their presence. In that frame, a few affected readings are enough for mathematics to derive a bound random number from the stored snapshot.

Plain facts

What this site can actually state

  • One Home Assistant EMF sensor is exposed through a public Cloudflare-cached API.
  • The feed can be polled, graphed, replayed across historical ranges, exported, and summarized with entropy.
  • The timeline combines technical markers with fictional story milestones drawn from the supplied prompts.
  • The narrative layer is kept to the stated story facts rather than extra invented framing.
  • The site still separates measurable data from story material so developers can inspect the feed directly.
